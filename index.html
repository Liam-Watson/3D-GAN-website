3D-GAN

A point cloud solution to 3D face generation using generative adversarial networks.

Overview

Problems encountered historically had to be solved by a hand crafted technique which has given rise to many efficient algorithms. However, such solutions can often be highly complex and difficult to implement. In recent years, the advent of deep learning has given rise to a new class of algorithms which can be trained to solve a wide range of problems.

Deep learning fields that involve classification or prediction problems have been highly studied, but, only recently have researchers started to focus on generative applications. Many generative models exist having displayed remarkable results in image generation, text generation, and music generation. Examples of such models include StyleGAN, WaveNet and DALL-E. However, such models have been limited to low dimensional generation tasks.

The solutions proposed in the three sibling papers introduced extend the remarkable generation capabilities of GANs to 3D point clouds. Each paper focuses on a different GAN architecture namely: Controllable GAN, Conditional GAN and Progressive GAN. The controllable and Conditional GANs achieve expression control while the progressive GAN seeks to achieve higher fidelity results.

Controllable GAN

A controllable GAN is designed to allow for fine-grained and incremental control over the GAN output. It does this by using a pre-trained classifier to inform changes to the noise input of the generator to explore the latent space and thus manipulate features. PCE-GAN is a controllable GAN where the generator is taken from a general GAN that can generate 3D faces, and the PointNet classifier is used to control traversal within latent space. The control was applied to the face's expression leaving the identity of the face uncontrolled. This allows generation of faces with a specific expression as well as expression strength control and temporally consistent expression interpolation. Conditional GAN

The 3D Expression Conditional GAN (3DEC-GAN) is a network designed to generate faces while applying coarse-grained control over the output. Conditional GANs function via the use of labels; the training data is labeled and the user is then able to provide the model with a label that defines some property of the output. In the case of 3DEC-GAN, the label value corresponds to the expression of the face. This resulted in a model which can generate a face with either neutral, mouth open, cheeks in, or bare teeth expression. It was achieved by concatenating conditional information vectors onto the generator and discriminator of a GAN that can generate a geneal 3D face.

Progressive GAN

Progressive GANs are built around the idea of progressive growing the GAN from producing low-resolution output to a high-resolution output. They learn a concept the same way we would learn a concept such as maths, starting out with the basics and building on them. The 3D-ProGAN starts out with a resolution of 512 vertices, which is then progressively grown to 1024 vertices. Where the number of vertices represents the size of the outputted point cloud. The GAN is progressively grown using skip connections, up-sampling and down-sampling. Adding a single progressive layer seemed to increase the quality of the face generated.
